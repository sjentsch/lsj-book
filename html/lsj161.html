3Oblique rotations provide two factor matrices, one called a structure matrix and one called a pattern matrix. In jamovi just the pattern matrix is shown in the results as this is typically the most useful for interpretation, though some experts suggest that both can be helpful. In a structure matrix coeﬃcients show the relationship between the variable and the factors whilst ignoring the relationship of that factor with all the other factors (i.e. a zero-order correlation). Pattern matrix coeﬃcients show the unique contribution of a factor to a variable whilst controlling for the eﬀects of other factors on that variable (akin to standardized partial regression coeﬃcient). Under orthogonal rotation, structure and pattern coeﬃcients are the same.
